Key Tips for Mika's MEXT Journey

01
Confirm IPA → IPS-B cross-track eligibility
Mika is IPA track but wants Economics (IPS-B). MEXT's application categories can sometimes cross, but it varies by cycle. Contact the Japanese Embassy in Jakarta (id.emb-japan.go.jp) in 2027 to clarify this before the 2028 window. Have IPA-A (e.g., Mathematics) ready as a fallback choice.
02
JLPT N2 is the real game-changer
N3 (July 2026) is the first meaningful bonus threshold. But N2 awards significantly more bonus points in MEXT scoring. Target N2 by July 2027 — two more attempts available (Dec 2026, Jul 2027, Dec 2027). Don't stop at N3.
03
Get IELTS done in 2026 — scores last 2 years
IELTS scores are valid for 2 years. A mid-2026 exam score will remain valid through mid-2028 — exactly covering the April 2028 MEXT application. If pushed to 2027 or later, the score may expire before the application or arrive too late.
04
SNBT is a free bonus — save the score report
The SNBT score (Grade XII, ~May 2027) is submitted voluntarily as a bonus in the MEXT application. Since Mika is taking it anyway, just save the official score report — no extra MEXT-specific preparation needed for this one.
05
Legalize documents in September 2027 — not April 2028
The Ijazah, rapor transcripts, recommendation letter, and school accreditation all need official legalization. Start this process in September 2027, right after receiving the diploma. Legalization can take several weeks and schools may have processing queues.
06
Know the target universities by name in Japanese
Top Economics universities in Japan: Hitotsubashi University (一橋大学, ranked #1 for Economics in Japan), University of Tokyo (東京大学), Kyoto University (京都大学), Osaka University (大阪大学). While MEXT assigns universities, knowing these by their Japanese names demonstrates seriousness during the interview.
