Strategic Tips for Indonesian High Schoolers

01
Start IELTS prep 6–12 months early
Beasiswa Garuda requires IELTS ≥ 6.5. NTU/NUS require strong English. Begin prep in Kelas 11 — IELTS scores are valid for 2 years and make every application stronger.
02
Apply to university AND scholarship simultaneously
Most scholarships here (KAIST, NTU, NUS, HKUST, SKKU) require no separate application — you're considered automatically when you apply for admission. Don't wait.
03
Secure a LoA before Beasiswa Garuda deadline
Beasiswa Garuda is the most accessible fully-funded option for Indonesian SMA students, but you MUST have a Letter of Acceptance. Apply to overseas universities by January–February to have the LoA ready for April.
04
Build your co-curricular portfolio strategically
NTU and NUS explicitly evaluate co-curricular records and leadership. National-level olympiad medals, student council leadership, or community projects significantly boost your profile.
05
Use Beasiswa Garuda as a safety net strategy
Apply directly to NTU/NUS/KAIST for their university scholarships first (Jan–Feb). If awarded a LoA, you can also independently apply for Beasiswa Garuda (April) to layer funding options.
06
Check QS rankings for eligible universities
Beasiswa Garuda requires universities to be in QS Top 100 (subject ranking). NUS, NTU, KAIST, HKUST, and SKKU all qualify. Cross-reference the official Kemdiktisaintek partner university list before applying.
